### [1] [Topology sums, sectorwise holography, and horizon normalcy](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20294)
*Naman Kumar*

Main category: hep-th

This challenges the expectation that horizon smoothness should depend only on local semiclassical geometry, unless the baby-universe Hilbert space $\mathcal H_{\rm BU}$ is trivial ($\dim=1$), as conjectured in some approaches to quantum gravity.
\n\n

### [2] [The Equivalence Principle at High Energies Completes the Spectrum](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20319)

TL;DR: The paper proves that in gravitational theories with a global symmetry, tree-level scattering and the equivalence principle imply the existence of single-particle states in every irreducible representation constructible from a seed charge; these states all couple with equal strength (“democratically”) to gravity.
\n\n
本文证明，在具有全局对称性的引力理论中，树图散射和等效原理共同蕴含了从一个种子荷出发可构造的所有不可约表示中均存在单粒子态；这些态以相等的耦合强度（“民主地”）参与引力相互作用。

The authors aim to explore how the equivalence principle—encoded as universal high-energy scattering behavior—in conjunction with symmetry, constrains the particle content of consistent gravitational theories.
\n\n
动机源于引力与全局对称性之间的相互作用：尽管精确的全局对称性被认为与量子引力不相容，但近似或涌现的对称性可能在粒子谱上留下印记。作者旨在探究等效原理（体现为高能散射行为的普适性）与对称性如何共同约束自洽引力理论中的粒子内容。

By demanding consistency of factorization and crossing symmetry in scattering amplitudes, they deduce that all irreducible representations obtainable via tensor products of the seed representation must be populated by physical single-particle states.
\n\n

Result: The main result is a proof of a completeness-type statement: if a gravitational theory contains one charged state under a symmetry group $G$, it must contain single-particle states in every irreducible representation of $G$ that can be built from that seed. Conclusion: This work strengthens the connection between the equivalence principle, symmetry, and spectral completeness in quantum gravity. It suggests that gravitational interactions not only forbid exact global symmetries but also enforce a rich and symmetric particle spectrum, with democratic coupling as a direct consequence of universality in high-energy scattering.
\n\n

Abstract: We prove a version of the completeness hypothesis that follows from the coexistence of symmetry and gravity: tree-level gravitational scattering mandates single-particle states in all possible irreducible representations of the symmetry group constructible from a single seed charge. Our main assumption is that the leading high-energy behavior of scattering is universal irrespective of charge, thus satisfying the equivalence principle.
