### [1] [On the impossibility of observational confirmation of black holes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13901)
*Thiago T. Bergamaschi*

Main category: gr-qc

TL;DR: The paper argues that despite strong observational consistency with Kerr black hole predictions from LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA, Event Horizon Telescope, and GRAVITY, current data only support the existence of black hole candidates—not definitive proof—due to fundamental limitations inherent in general relativity itself regarding what can be observationally confirmed.
\n\n
本文指出，尽管LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA、Event Horizon Telescope和GRAVITY的观测数据与Kerr黑洞预言高度一致，但由于广义相对论本身对可观测性存在根本限制，当前数据仅能支持“黑洞候选体”的存在，而非确凿证明黑洞真实存在。

  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to clarify a widespread misconception in the astrophysics community and public discourse: that recent high-precision observations have definitively proven the existence of black holes. The paper emphasizes that such claims overstate the epistemic reach of empirical data within the framework of general relativity, which inherently restricts what can be verified about event horizons and spacetime singularities.
\n\n
动机在于澄清天体物理学界和公众讨论中的一个普遍误解：即近期高精度观测已确凿证明黑洞存在。本文强调，此类主张夸大了在广义相对论框架内经验数据的认知能力，而该理论本身对事件视界和时空奇点的可验证性存在固有限制。

Method: The analysis is conceptual and foundational, relying on logical and epistemological reasoning grounded in the structure of general relativity. It examines the nature of observational evidence (e.g., gravitational waves, shadow imaging, stellar orbits) and demonstrates that all such data are consistent with—but not uniquely diagnostic of—true black holes, as alternative compact objects without event horizons can mimic the same signatures within observational uncertainties.
\n\n
该分析是概念性和基础性的，基于广义相对论结构内的逻辑与认识论推理。它考察了观测证据（如引力波、阴影成像、恒星轨道）的本质，并论证所有此类数据虽与真实黑洞一致，但并非其唯一诊断特征，因为不含事件视界的其他致密天体可在观测误差范围内模拟相同信号。

Conclusion: The paper concludes that black holes remain theoretical constructs whose physical reality cannot be empirically established beyond doubt due to fundamental limitations imposed by general relativity itself. \n\n

Abstract: General relativity has achieved remarkable experimental and observational success. While current data are in striking agreement with the predictions for Kerr black holes, they can only rule out specific alternative models of compact objects rather than provide conclusive proof of black holes. More fundamentally, and independent of whether or not black holes exist, general relativity itself imposes limits on what can be observationally established. Essentially, no observational data is sufficient to confirm the existence of black holes.

Abstract: Heavy spin-one particles are well-motivated new physics candidates that can have their origin in weakly coupled extensions of the Standard Model gauge group or in strongly coupled Composite Higgs models. Due to the variety of production and decay modes, heavy vector triplets are a useful benchmark for the study and comparison of future colliders. Here we perform a detailed collider analysis of a variety of $2 \to 2$ and $2 \to 3$ processes at a proposed future muon collider. We focus on decays into leptons and Standard Model gauge bosons, and find that heavy vector triplets could be probed up to masses of around $12\,$TeV for almost any (perturbative) value of the coupling. We compare the direct reach of a muon collider to the LHC and to updated projections for the HL-LHC, HE-LHC and FCC-hh, and include indirect limits from future measurements of electroweak precision observables. We find that a muon collider offers projected sensitivities that are competitive with future hadron colliders, exceeding those of the HE-LHC in the scenarios considered though not reaching the projected sensitivity of the FCC-hh.
