Method: Derives the relation $L = L_0 \exp(N) \left( \frac{\rho_{\text{reh}}}{\rho_{\text{infl}}} \right)^{\alpha}$ where $L$ is the current universe size, $N$ is inflation e-folds, $\rho_{\text{reh}}$ and $\rho_{\text{infl}}$ are reheating and inflation energy densities, and $\alpha$ is a constant determined by the theory's particle content.

推导关系式 $L = L_0 \exp(N) \left( \frac{\rho_{\text{reh}}}{\rho_{\text{infl}}} \right)^{\alpha}$，其中 $L$ 为当前宇宙尺度，$N$ 为通胀 e 折叠数，$\rho_{\text{reh}}$ 和 $\rho_{\text{infl}}$ 分别为再加热与通胀能量密度，$\alpha$ 由理论粒子内容确定的常数。

Result: The three-torus universe achieves $L \sim 10 \, H_0^{-1}$ at present, consistent with CMB anomalies, and the relation constrains $N$ and $\rho_{\text{reh}}$ to satisfy Planck's lower bound on universe size.

three-torus 宇宙当前尺度 $L \sim 10 \, H_0^{-1}$ 与 CMB 异常一致，该关系式约束了 $N$ 和 $\rho_{\text{reh}}$ 以满足 Planck 对宇宙尺度的下限。

Conclusion: The model resolves the low-multipole CMB anomaly by matching the universe's present size to the three-torus topology, with parameters constrained by Casimir energy-driven pre-inflationary evolution.

该模型通过将宇宙当前尺度与 three-torus 拓扑匹配，利用卡西米尔能量驱动的通胀前演化约束参数，解决低多极 CMB 异常。

Abstract: We consider a Universe with a three-torus topology which before inflation is devoid of any matter or radiation. Its pre-inflationary evolution is driven solely by Casimir energies of the existing fields, with a radiation-like equation of state. We show that, quite strikingly, with the appropriate number of fermions and bosons in the theory, such a Universe evolving from Planck size at Planck time has the correct critical energy density today. Moreover, assuming typical parameters for inflation and reheating, the three-torus Universe can be several Hubble radii across at present time, which is precisely the size hinted by the anomaly in the cosmic microwave background observed at low multipole moments. We derive a relation between the size of the Universe, the number of e-folds of inflation, and the energy density decrease during reheating, which we then use to determine the parameter values consistent with the anomaly and the lower bound on the size of the Universe from Planck.
