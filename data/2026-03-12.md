*Siddhartha Bandyopadhyay,Ujjal Kumar Dey*

Main category: hep-ph

TL;DR: This study calculates the Stodolsky effect (CνB detection) using general neutrino-electron interactions beyond SM, finding only NSI and tensor terms contribute beyond SM results for both Dirac/Majorana neutrinos.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The Cosmic Neutrino Background (CνB) detection via the Stodolsky effect requires understanding neutrino-electron interactions beyond Standard Model physics to assess sensitivity to new physics contributions.

Method: Employed most general Lorentz-invariant neutrino-electron interactions respecting SU(3)⊗U(1) symmetry below electroweak scale, calculated for both Dirac and Majorana neutrino cases.

Result: Only non-standard neutrino interactions (NSI) and tensor interaction terms contribute beyond Standard Model to Stodolsky effect; scalar/pseudoscalar terms vanish identically.

Conclusion: CνB detection via Stodolsky effect is sensitive to NSI and tensor interactions, providing probe of beyond-SM physics independent of neutrino mass hierarchy details.

Abstract: We study the Stodolsky effect utilizing the most general form of neutrino interactions with electrons below the electroweak scale by considering all possible Lorentz invariant operators respecting SU(3)$\otimes$U(1) symmetry. We perform our calculation for both Dirac and Majorana neutrinos and find that in the most general setting, only the non-standard neutrino interactions and the tensor interaction terms provide a non-zero contribution, apart from the Standard Model contribution. We investigate the implications for the possible detection of the cosmic neutrino background (C$ν$B) by analysing the energy shifts that are characteristic of the Stodolsky effect. We also discuss the implication of considerable asymmetry in the C$ν$B on the present scenario.

Motivation: Holographic conformal field theories at finite temperature exhibit "bouncing singularities" in complex time in their thermal correlators, which are believed to be related to bulk geodesics probing the black hole interior. These singularities appear as exponentially suppressed contributions in the high-frequency limit. The paper aims to understand the universal nature of these singularities by comparing two independent approaches: one sensitive to black hole interior physics (WKB) and another relying only on boundary data (OPE).

Method: Two complementary approaches are employed: (1) Large frequency WKB analysis with infalling boundary conditions at the horizon, which probes the black hole interior through bulk geodesics. (2) Asymptotic OPE analysis using only the near-boundary expansion without direct input from black hole interior. Both methods are applied to: (i) retarded two-point functions of local operators dual to bulk scalar fields in planar AdS black hole background, and (ii) retarded two-point functions of displacement operators on Wilson lines (defect correlators) computed holographically via wave equation for transverse fluctuations of dual string worldsheet.

Result: The analysis confirms that both bulk scalar correlators and defect correlators on Wilson lines exhibit bouncing singularities. Crucially, exact agreement is found between the WKB analysis (sensitive to black hole interior) and asymptotic OPE analysis (relying only on near-boundary data). This agreement suggests that bouncing singularities and corresponding OPE data encode universal high-frequency structure of retarded correlators. A factorization formula is proposed that encodes deviations from this universality.

Conclusion: Bouncing singularities in holographic thermal correlators represent a universal high-frequency feature that can be accessed through two complementary methods. The agreement between interior-sensitive WKB analysis and boundary-only OPE analysis reveals that these singularities encode information about black hole interior without requiring explicit interior data. The universality extends from bulk operators to defect operators, suggesting a factorization structure for deviations from this universal behavior.

Abstract: Thermal correlators in holographic conformal field theories are known to exhibit singularities in complex time, sometimes referred to as ``bouncing singularities", which are believed to be related to bulk geodesics probing the black hole interior. These singularities correspond to exponentially suppressed contributions in the high-frequency limit of the thermal correlators. We revisit in detail the calculation of retarded two-point functions of local operators dual to bulk scalar fields in the planar AdS black hole background. We confirm that these correlators develop bouncing singularities, and highlight the agreement of two independent methods: a large frequency WKB analysis with infalling boundary conditions at the horizon; and an asymptotic OPE analysis that relies only on the near-boundary expansion, without any direct input from the black hole interior. We then extend these calculations to the case of the retarded two-point function of displacement operators on a Wilson line in the finite temperature gauge theory. This is computed holographically by solving the wave equation for the transverse fluctuations of the dual string worldsheet in the planar AdS black hole background. We find that these defect correlators also exhibit bouncing singularities, and again observe exact agreement between the WKB analysis sensitive to the black hole interior and the asymptotic OPE analysis. This agreement suggests that the bouncing singularities and the corresponding OPE data encode a universal high-frequency structure of the retarded correlators, and we propose a factorization formula that encodes the deviations from this universality.
