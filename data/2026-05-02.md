### [1] [Mapping data sensitivities in global QCD analysis with linear response and influence functions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28154)
*Richard Whitehill*

Main category: hep-ph

TL;DR: The paper introduces a framework using linear response and influence functions to better understand how experimental data constrains non-perturbative functions in QCD, providing a transparent method for diagnosing information flow in inverse problems.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Global QCD analyses are crucial for extracting hadron structure from experimental data, but the high dimensionality and complexity of these fits make it difficult to interpret how data constrains non-perturbative functions. The motivation is to develop a more interpretable and transparent framework for understanding this process.

全局QCD分析是从未知实验数据中提取强子结构的主要框架，但由于这些拟合的高维度和复杂性，使得如何通过数据约束非微扰函数变得难以解释。动机是开发一个更易于解释和透明的框架来理解这一过程。

Method: The authors develop a framework based on linear response and influence functions, which are gradient-based sensitivity measures. These measures directly quantify how experimental information propagates to fitted quantities and observables, thereby exposing how data locally determines the central values, uncertainties, and correlations of quantum correlation functions.

作者基于线性响应和影响函数开发了一个框架，这些是基于梯度的灵敏度测量方法。这些方法直接量化了实验信息如何传播到拟合量和可观测量，从而揭示了数据如何局部确定量子关联函数的中心值、不确定性和相关性。

Result: The framework provides a transparent and general method for diagnosing information flow in inverse problems in QCD. It allows for a clearer understanding of how experimental data constrains non-perturbative functions, including the central values, uncertainties, and correlations between them.

该框架提供了一种透明且通用的方法来诊断QCD中的逆问题的信息流。它使人们能够更清楚地了解实验数据如何约束非微扰函数，包括它们的中心值、不确定性和相关性。

Conclusion: The developed framework offers a new, transparent, and general approach to diagnose information flow in global QCD analyses, enabling a better understanding of how experimental data constrains non-perturbative functions and their uncertainties and correlations.

所开发的框架为诊断全局QCD分析中的信息流提供了一种新的、透明且通用的方法，使人们能够更好地理解实验数据如何约束非微扰函数及其不确定性和相关性。

Abstract: Global QCD analyses provide the primary framework for extracting hadron structure from experimental data, yet the mechanisms by which data constrain non-perturbative functions remain difficult to interpret due to the high dimensionality and complexity of these fits. Here we develop a framework based on linear response and influence functions, which are gradient-based sensitivity measures that directly quantify how experimental information propagates to fitted quantities and observables. These quantities cleanly expose how data locally determines the central values and uncertainties of quantum correlation functions, as well as the correlations between them, providing a transparent and general framework for diagnosing information flow in inverse problems in QCD.

### [2] [Deeply virtual pion production through two-loop order](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28164)
*Wen Chen,Feng Feng,Yu Jia,Qing-Tao Song,Guang Tang,Zhe-Yu Wang*

Main category: hep-ph

TL;DR: This paper presents the first calculation of next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) QCD radiative corrections to deeply virtual meson production (DVMP) processes, specifically $\gamma_L^* p \to \pi^+ n$ and $\gamma_L^* p \to \pi^0 p$, in the generalized Bjorken limit. The two-loop QCD corrections significantly improve the agreement between theoretical predictions and JLab data, and their impact on transverse single-spin asymmetries (TSSA) is also studied for future experiments at JLab, EIC, and EicC.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation for this work is to provide a more accurate theoretical framework for understanding the nucleon's generalized parton distributions (GPDs) through deeply virtual meson production (DVMP) processes. Accurate extraction of GPDs is crucial for unraveling the three-dimensional internal structure of the nucleon, which is a key goal in hadronic physics. The study aims to bridge the gap between theoretical predictions and experimental data by including higher-order QCD corrections.

Method: The authors employ the collinear factorization framework to calculate the next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) QCD radiative corrections to the DV$\\pi$P processes $\\gamma_L^* p \\to \\pi^+ n$ and $\\gamma_L^* p \\to \\pi^0 p$. These calculations are performed in the generalized Bjorken limit, where $Q^2 \\gg |t|, \\Lambda_{\\text{QCD}}^2$. The method involves evaluating the two-loop QCD corrections and assessing their impact on the differential longitudinal DV$\\pi$P cross section and transverse single-spin asymmetries (TSSA).

Result: The results show that the two-loop QCD corrections have a positive and substantial impact on the DV$\\pi$P processes. Including these corrections significantly improves the agreement between the perturbative QCD prediction and the available JLab data. Additionally, the study demonstrates the influence of the two-loop QCD corrections on the TSSA in benchmark kinematics at JLab, EIC, and EicC, providing valuable insights for future experimental analyses.

Conclusion: The inclusion of next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) QCD radiative corrections in the calculation of deeply virtual meson production (DVMP) processes enhances the precision of theoretical predictions. This improved accuracy leads to better agreement with experimental data from JLab and provides a more reliable framework for future experiments at EIC and EicC. The study underscores the importance of higher-order QCD corrections in understanding the nucleon's internal structure through GPDs.

Abstract: Deeply virtual meson production (DVMP) is among the most prominent channels to extract the nucleon's generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at $ep$ scattering facilities such as {\tt JLab} and the upcoming {\tt EIC/EicC} experiments, which plays a vital role in unravelling the three-dimensional internal structure of nucleon. In this work we calculate for the first time the next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) QCD radiative corrections to the DV$π$P processes $γ_L^* p\to π^+ n$ and $γ_L^* p\to π^0 p$ in the generalized Bjorken limit $Q^2\gg \vert t\vert, Λ_{\text{QCD}}^2$, accurate at the leading twist within collinear factorization framework. The impact of the two-loop QCD corrections appears to be positive and substantial, including which considerably improves the agreement between the perturbative QCD prediction and the available {\tt JLab} data. In addition to the differential longitudinal DV$π$P cross section, we also study the impact of the two-loop QCD corrections on the transverse single-spin asymmetries (TSSA) in some benchmark kinematics at {\tt JLab}, {\tt EIC} and {\tt EicC}.
